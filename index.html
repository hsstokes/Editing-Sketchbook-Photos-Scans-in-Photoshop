Editing Photos & Scans of Physical Work in Photoshop 🖼️

1️⃣ Capture Your Sketchbook Work

You can record physical pages two ways — both have benefits.

✨ Option A: Scan at College

  • Use the college printers/scanners — place your page flat on the glass and close the lid gently.
  • Swipe your student card and choose Scan to Email.
  • Set resolution to 300 dpi to keep detail sharp.
  • Watch for washed‑out paper; Pencil or light graphite may scan faintly.
  • Scanned files arrive in your inbox — download them to your drive or desktop.

📸 Option B: Photograph Your Pages

  • Use soft daylight (not direct sunlight or yellow bulbs).
  • Lay the sketchbook flat; stand or mount your phone above it.
  • Take several shots and pick the most evenly lit one.
  • Photos often capture surface texture that scanners miss.
💡 Best results: combine both approaches — scan for clarity + photograph for texture.

2️⃣ Open and Duplicate the Layer

  • Open the image in Photoshop.
  • In the Layers panel, drag the Background layer to the “New Layer” icon to make a copy.
  • This protects your original if you need to revert.

3️⃣ Adjust Light and Colour with the Camera Raw Filter

  • Go to Filter → Camera Raw Filter…
  • Under the right‑hand Color section, change the White Balance menu to Custom.
  • Use the sliders to refine the paper tone:
    • Temperature: Left = cooler (blue) · Right = warmer (yellow)
    • Tint: Left = green · Right = magenta
  • Small adjustments are best — the goal is clean white paper, not brilliant white.
  • Click OK to apply and return to Photoshop.
🪄 Keep it simple: no eyedropper needed — just Temperature + Tint in the Camera Raw Filter for natural balance.

4️⃣ Selecting or Placing into an A3 Layout

  • To move part of a page, choose the Rectangular Marquee Tool (M) → select → Ctrl/Cmd + C → paste into your new A3 document.
  • Or, in your A3 file, use File → Place Embedded … to insert the whole page as a flexible layer.
  • Resize or reposition with Ctrl/Cmd + T (Free Transform).
🧩 No need to crop: keep full pages — you can frame or mask them later within your A3 compositions.

5️⃣ Save Clearly

  • Go to File → Export → Save for Web (Legacy).
  • Select JPEG High Quality (80–90%) for Moodle / Padlet uploads.
  • Rename your file clearly: firstname_sketchbook_edit.jpg.
  • Keep file size under 2 MB for easy sharing.
