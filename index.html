Editing Photos & Scans of Physical Work in Photoshop 🖼️

1️⃣ Capture Your Sketchbook Work

You can record physical pages two ways — both have benefits.

✨ Option A — Scan at College

  • Use the college printers/scanners — place your page flat on the glass and close the lid gently.
  • Swipe your student card and choose Scan to Email.
  • Set resolution to 300 dpi for sharp detail.
  • Watch for washed‑out paper; pencil may scan faintly.

📸 Option B — Photograph Your Pages

  • Use soft daylight (not direct sunlight).
  • Lay flat; stand or mount your phone above the page.
  • Take several shots and pick the most evenly lit one.
💡 Best results: combine both approaches — scan for clarity + photograph for texture.

2️⃣ Open & Duplicate Layers

  • Open your image in Photoshop.
  • Drag the Background layer onto the New Layer icon.
  • This keeps a safe original beneath.

3️⃣ Adjust Light & Colour — Camera Raw Filter

  • Filter → Camera Raw Filter…
  • Set White Balance = Custom.
  • Use sliders:
    • Temperature: left = cool, right = warm
    • Tint: left = green, right = magenta
  • Aim for clean white paper, not pure white.
🪄 Keep it simple: Temperature + Tint give natural balance — no eyedropper needed.

4️⃣ Place into A3 Layout

  • Select with Marquee Tool (M) → Ctrl/Cmd + C / Ctrl/Cmd + V.
  • Or File → Place Embedded… to insert your full page.
  • Resize via Ctrl/Cmd + T (Free Transform).
🧩 No need to crop — keep the page whole and frame it later.

5️⃣ Save Clearly

  • File → Export → Save for Web (Legacy)
  • Choose JPEG High Quality (80–90%) for Moodle / Padlet.
  • Name file firstname_sketchbook_edit.jpg.
  • Keep under 2 MB.
💾 Tip: Save a layered PSD copy for future editing.
